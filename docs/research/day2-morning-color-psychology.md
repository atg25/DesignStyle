# Mid-Century Modern Color Theory

## Historical Color Periods

### Early Period (1945-1955)
**Context:** Post-war conservatism, material shortages, limited pigments

**Colors:**
- Warm neutrals: beige, taupe, cream, gray
- Muted earth tones: sage, rust, coffee brown
- Pastels: mint, dusty rose, powder blue, butter yellow
- Natural wood: oak, maple, birch

### Peak Period (1955-1965) - Atomic Age
**Context:** Economic boom, Sputnik, consumer culture, space race optimism

**Colors:**
- Atomic brights: **orange #ff6b35**, **teal #1fb7c8**, yellow #f7b801, chartreuse
- Primary colors: red, blue, yellow
- Jewel tones: emerald, sapphire, amethyst
- Metallics: chrome silver, gold, copper

**Iconic Pairing:** Orange + Teal (complementary, high contrast, THE MCM combo)

### Late Period (1965-1970) - Earth Tones
**Context:** Vietnam War, environmental movement, counter-culture, reaction to "plastic" 60s

**Colors:**
- Avocado green #568203 & harvest gold #da9100 (dominant duo)
- Earth browns: chocolate, caramel, tan, rust
- Muted organics: olive, burnt orange, terracotta
- Natural neutrals: cream, off-white

## Color Theory Principles

### Complementary Colors
- Orange ↔ Teal (classic MCM)
- Red ↔ Green
- Yellow ↔ Purple
- Maximum contrast, vibrant, balanced

### Warm vs Cool
**Warm:** Orange, red, yellow, rust, gold (energetic, cozy, advancing)
**Cool:** Teal, blue, green, gray (calm, spacious, receding)
**MCM Innovation:** Balance both in same space

### 60-30-10 Rule

**White Dominant (Early-Mid):**
- 60% White/cream (walls, ceiling)
- 30% Natural wood (floor, furniture)
- 10% Bold color (orange sofa, teal chair)

**Wood Dominant (Peak):**
- 60% Natural wood (paneling, floor, furniture)
- 30% Neutral (white, gray, beige)
- 10% Bright accent (chartreuse, orange)
