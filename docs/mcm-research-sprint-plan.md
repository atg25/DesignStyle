# Mid-Century Modern Design Research Sprint Plan

**Project:** Mid-Century Modern Educational Website  
**Sprint Duration:** 2 Weeks (10 Working Days)  
**Goal:** Become MCM design experts capable of authentic design execution and comprehensive content creation  
**Team Outcome:** Deep understanding of MCM principles, visual language, historical context, and ability to design/code authentic MCM website

---

## Sprint Overview

### Week 1: Foundation & Immersion

**Focus:** Historical context, design principles, visual vocabulary

### Week 2: Deep Dive & Application

**Focus:** Designers, furniture, materials, and design system development

---

## Daily Research Schedule

### Day 1: Historical Context & Foundation (Monday Week 1)

#### Morning Session (3 hours): Origins & Philosophy

**Research Topics:**

- [ ] Post-WWII context (1945-1949)

  - Economic boom and suburban expansion
  - New materials from war innovation (plywood, plastics, aluminum)
  - Housing shortage and need for affordable, functional design
  - Optimism and modernism movement

- [ ] Core Philosophy: "Form Follows Function"
  - Bauhaus influence on American design
  - Rejection of ornate Victorian/Art Deco styles
  - Emphasis on simplicity and practicality
  - Democratic design (good design for everyone)

**Key Resources:**

- [ ] Read: "Mid-Century Modern: Furniture of the 1950s" by Cara Greenberg
- [ ] Watch: Documentary "Eames: The Architect & The Painter" (85 min)
- [ ] Browse: MoMA Mid-Century Modern collection online
- [ ] Article: "What is Mid-Century Modern Design?" - Architectural Digest

**Deliverable:** Timeline document (1945-1970) with key events, innovations, cultural context

#### Afternoon Session (3 hours): Geographic Variations

**Research Topics:**

- [ ] American MCM
  - West Coast modernism (California Case Study Houses)
  - East Coast interpretation
  - Suburban ranch houses
- [ ] Scandinavian Modern (Closely related but distinct)
  - Danish modern (Arne Jacobsen, Hans Wegner)
  - Finnish design (Alvar Aalto)
  - Swedish functionalism
- [ ] Compare & Contrast:
  - What makes American MCM unique?

**Key Resources:**

- [ ] Browse: Design Within Reach MCM collection

### Day 2: Color Theory & Palettes (Tuesday Week 1)

#### Morning Session (3 hours): Color Psychology

**Research Topics:**

- [ ] Early Period Colors (1945-1955)
  - Earth tones and muted colors
  - Beige, taupe, gray
  - Post-war conservatism in color
- [ ] Peak MCM Colors (1955-1965)
  - Bold, optimistic colors emerge
  - Orange, teal, mustard yellow
  - Burnt sienna, avocado green
  - Atomic age influence (space age colors)
- [ ] Late Period Colors (1965-1970)
  - Harvest gold, avocado green dominance
  - Burnt orange, chocolate brown
  - Transition toward 1970s earthiness

**Key Resources:**

- [ ] Book: "The Coloring Book" by Karen Michel
- [ ] Website: Authentic Mid-Century Modern color palettes
- [ ] Pinterest: "1950s color schemes" and "1960s interior colors"
- [ ] Tool: Adobe Color - analyze MCM photography

**Activities:**

- [ ] Extract color palettes from 20 authentic MCM interiors
- [ ] Document hex codes and color names
- [ ] Identify common color combinations
- [ ] Note which colors were used where (walls vs accents vs furniture)

**Deliverable:** MCM Color Palette Guide with:

- 10 authentic color schemes

- Usage guidelines (primary, secondary, accent)
- Period-specific notes

### Day 3: Typography & Visual Language (Wednesday Week 1)

#### Morning Session (3 hours): Typography Research

**Research Topics:**

- [ ] Iconic MCM Fonts
  - Futura (geometric sans-serif, most associated with MCM)
  - Helvetica (clean, neutral, introduced 1957)
  - Univers (Swiss design influence)
  - Century Gothic (American alternative)
  - Avant Garde Gothic (1970, late MCM)
- [ ] Display & Decorative Fonts
  - Script fonts (occasional use in advertising)
  - Atomic/space age lettering
  - Custom logotypes
- [ ] Typography Principles
  - Clean, legible sans-serifs preferred
  - Geometric shapes (circles, rectangles)
  - Large, bold headlines
  - Generous spacing/white space
  - Minimal use of serifs (when used: Garamond, Baskerville)

### Day 4: Furniture Icons & Designers (Thursday Week 1)

#### Morning Session (3 hours): Iconic Furniture Pieces

**Research Topics - Must-Know Pieces:**

**Seating:**

- [ ] **Eames Lounge Chair & Ottoman** (1956)
  - Materials: Molded plywood, leather, aluminum
  - Design principle: Comfort + elegance
  - Cultural impact: Appeared on Frasier TV show
- [ ] **Eames Molded Plastic Chair** (1950)
  - First mass-produced plastic chair
  - Shell chair design
  - Multiple base options
- [ ] **Barcelona Chair** (Mies van der Rohe, 1929, adopted by MCM)
  - Stainless steel frame
  - Leather cushions
  - Timeless design
- [ ] **Womb Chair** (Eero Saarinen, 1946)
  - Enveloping, organic form
  - Fabric upholstery
  - Psychological comfort
- [ ] **Egg Chair** (Arne Jacobsen, 1958)
  - Sculptural, organic shape
  - Swivel base
  - Danish modern influence
- [ ] **Tulip Chair** (Eero Saarinen, 1955-1956)
  - Single pedestal base
  - Futuristic design
  - No "slum of legs"

**Tables:**

- [ ] **Noguchi Coffee Table** (1947)
  - Sculptural wood base
  - Glass top
  - Biomorphic design
- [ ] **Tulip Table** (Saarinen)
  - Single pedestal
  - Various sizes
  - Timeless elegance

**Storage:**

- [ ] **Nelson Thin Edge Cabinets** (George Nelson)
  - Minimal, rectilinear
  - Tapered legs
  - Modular design
- [ ] **Eames Storage Units** (ESU)
  - Colorful, modular
  - Industrial materials
  - Customizable configuration

**Activities:**

- [ ] Create detailed notes on each piece
- [ ] Document materials, dimensions, design principles
- [ ] Find high-quality images from multiple angles
- [ ] Note current manufacturers/reproductions

**Deliverable:** Furniture encyclopedia with:

- 20 iconic pieces documented
- Designer attribution
- Historical context
- Design analysis

#### Afternoon Session (3 hours): Designer Deep Dive

**Must-Know Designers:**

- [ ] **Charles & Ray Eames** (Husband-wife team)
  - Philosophy: "The best for the most for the least"
  - Key works: Lounge chair, molded plywood chairs, ESU
  - Background: Architecture, film, exhibition design
  - Legacy: Most influential MCM designers
- [ ] **Eero Saarinen**
  - Philosophy: Sculptural, organic modernism
  - Key works: Tulip chair/table, Womb chair, TWA Terminal
  - Background: Finnish-American architect
  - Quote: "Always design a thing by considering it in its next larger context"
- [ ] **George Nelson**
  - Role: Design director at Herman Miller (1945-1972)
  - Key works: Marshmallow sofa, Ball clock, Platform bench
  - Legacy: Curator of MCM, promoted Eames and others
- [ ] **Arne Jacobsen** (Danish)
  - Key works: Egg chair, Swan chair, Series 7 chair
  - Background: Architect and designer
  - Style: Organic forms, Scandinavian minimalism
- [ ] **Hans Wegner** (Danish)
  - Known for: Wishbone chair, Shell chair
  - Philosophy: Master of wood joinery
  - Legacy: "The master of the chair"
- [ ] **Florence Knoll**
  - Role: Knoll Associates director
  - Key works: Credenzas, tables, office furniture
  - Impact: Brought modernism to corporate America
- [ ] **Isamu Noguchi** (Sculptor-designer)
  - Key works: Coffee table, Akari lamps
  - Style: Biomorphic, sculptural
  - Background: Japanese-American artist

**Activities:**

- [ ] Create designer profile for each
- [ ] Document design philosophy and notable quotes
- [ ] Collect portfolio of their best works (5-10 pieces each)
- [ ] Note relationships (who worked with whom)
- [ ] Identify signature styles

**Deliverable:** Designer profiles document with:

- Biography (brief)
- Design philosophy
- Signature pieces
- Visual style characteristics
- Timeline of major works

#### Evening Session (1 hour): Video Deep Dive

- [ ] Watch: "Abstract: The Art of Design" - Bjarke Ingels (MCM influence)
- [ ] Watch: "Eames: The Architect & The Painter" (continued)
- [ ] YouTube: Herman Miller factory tours (historical footage)

**Deliverable:** Notes on manufacturing processes and design thinking

## Tips for Effective Research

### Note-Taking Strategy

1. **Use consistent format:**

   - Topic
   - Key points (bullet list)
   - Sources
   - Personal observations
   - Questions for further research

2. **Create visual references:**

   - Screenshots with annotations
   - Pinterest boards by category
   - Figma mood boards
   - Color palette files

3. **Document sources:**
   - Keep bibliography
   - Note page numbers
   - Save URLs with timestamps
   - Screenshot important passages

### Time Management

- Set timers for each session
- Take 10-minute breaks every hour
- Don't get lost in rabbit holes (set research boundaries)
- Prioritize breadth first, depth second
- Review daily progress each evening

### Collaboration

- Share discoveries in team chat
- Create shared Google Drive/Dropbox
- Use collaborative tools (Figma, Miro)
- Schedule daily sync (15 minutes)
- Divide and conquer topics

### Quality Over Quantity

- Better to deeply understand 10 pieces than superficially know 100
- Focus on "why" not just "what"
- Connect dots between research areas
- Look for patterns and themes
- Question assumptions
